Monarch Butterfly

Monarch Butterflies are identified by their bright orange wings, black veins and spots; which warn predators of their poisonous nature.

  • Scientific Name: Danus Plexippus
  • Average Length: 90-100mm
  • Average Lifespan: 4-5 weeks
  • Habitat: Meadows and grasslands

Monarch Butterflies are native to North and South America. In early Fall they migrate from the United States and Canada to California and Mexico. Monarch's are the only butterflies that make a two-way migration. This migration process spans over four generations of Monarchs each year. The first 3 generations of Monarch Butterflies have a life cycle of 4-5 weeks; whereas the forth generation may live up to 9 months.
